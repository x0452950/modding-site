Nexus Tablet Rooting & Firmware Guide

Overview

This guide covers everything from preparing your Nexus device to downgrading firmware, rooting, and installing custom recovery. Tested on various Nexus tablets with successful results.

Tools Used

  • ADB & Fastboot
  • Custom Recovery (TWRP)
  • Rooting Packages (SuperSU, Magisk)
  • Odin (for specific Samsung/Nexus builds)
  • Google USB Drivers & Nexus Toolkit (optional)

Preparation

  • Enable Developer Options by tapping build number 7 times
  • Turn on OEM Unlocking and USB Debugging
  • Install latest ADB drivers
  • Use adb devices to confirm your device is detected

Firmware Downgrading

  • Download the correct factory image from Google's official Nexus archive
  • Extract and flash components manually via Fastboot
  • Commands used: fastboot flash bootloader, fastboot flash system, etc.
  • Odin may be used for models based on Samsung firmware architecture

Unlocking Bootloader

  • Reboot to fastboot mode and use: fastboot oem unlock
  • Device will reset — back up important data beforehand

Installing TWRP & Gaining Root

  • Flash TWRP: fastboot flash recovery twrp.img
  • Boot into recovery: adb reboot recovery
  • Transfer and flash SuperSU or Magisk ZIP inside TWRP

Post-Root Setup

  • Verify root using Root Checker from the Play Store
  • Install root tools like Titanium Backup, AdAway, and Greenify
  • Use TWRP to create full NANDroid backups before experimenting further

Additional Notes

  • Always match firmware to your device model (Wi-Fi vs LTE)
  • Double-check checksum for downloaded images to avoid bricking
  • For soft bricks, bootloader + image flashing usually recovers the device
All guides are for educational purposes only. Use your powers responsibly.
